<section class="home-intro-card" markdown="1">
I am a student in the ninth cohort of the [Master of Behavioral and Decision Sciences](https://www.lps.upenn.edu/degree-programs/mbds) at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by [Prof. Lorin Hitt](https://oid.wharton.upenn.edu/profile/lhitt/) at the Wharton School. I received my BSc (Hons) in Information Management and Information Systems from [Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University (XJTLU)](https://www.xjtlu.edu.cn/en) and the University of Liverpool in 2025, advised by [Prof. Xuezhong (Tony) He](https://scholar.xjtlu.edu.cn/en/persons/XuezhongHe/). My research has been presented at premier conferences including PACIS, AMCIS, INFORMS, and AOM.

My academic training spans information systems, finance, and computer science. I currently collaborate with [Dr. Jiding Zhang](https://jidingz.github.io/) at Arizona State University and [Dr. Simin Li](https://siminli.wp.tulane.edu/) at Tulane University on AI-generated review summaries and their implications for information provision on digital platforms. In parallel, I work with [Dr. Bingjie Deng](https://scholar.xjtlu.edu.cn/en/persons/BingjieDeng/) at XJTLU, continuing a collaboration from my undergraduate studies that applies experimental methods to human–computer interaction and user behavior in online environments. Earlier, I conducted research on large language models for market information analysis and prediction in the Department of Finance at XJTLU under [Dr. Jia Zhai](https://scholar.xjtlu.edu.cn/en/persons/JiaZhai) and [Dr. Shimeng Shi](https://scholar.xjtlu.edu.cn/en/persons/ShimengShi/), and contributed to core components of large language model training pipelines under [Prof. Dapeng Tao](https://www.ise.ynu.edu.cn/faculty/68/) at Yunnan University, which strengthened my foundation in applied machine learning. I also completed the Research Seminar in Information Systems at the Wharton School, focused on the economics of information systems.

My research focuses on the economics of digital platforms and artificial intelligence, examining how algorithmic systems shape both information environments and market outcomes. In particular, I study how advances in generative AI and platform algorithms influence the production, aggregation, and visibility of information, as well as pricing, allocation, and competition on digital platforms, and how these changes affect consumer behavior and market structure. My recent work investigates how AI driven content generation and curation alter the salience and distribution of user generated information, and how these changes affect online word of mouth, user engagement, and reputation formation. I also examine how micro level informational cues such as linguistic framing, tags, and emojis interact with algorithmic systems to shape consumer responses. Methodologically, I combine econometric analysis with machine learning techniques and large language model based annotation, and complement these approaches with online and field experiments to identify causal mechanisms and behavioral responses.
